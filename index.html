Public Prayers

A prayer has traditionally been a message to God that you utter with your voice, or think silently in your head. But in a world where I talk to people through digital text more often than I do with my voice, I feel like it might be interesting to try a new form of prayer that reflects how many people really 'talk'. God is omniscient (all-knowing). He knows what we pray before we even pray it. He has access to every database, including this one :)

Matthew 6:5-6 makes a point that prayers are not supposed to be flashy public shows of piety. I am aware that my sinful heart could twist this blog into a modern rendition of that ancient hypocrisy. Therefore, the majority of prayers in this system will be kept on private, accessible only through a secret URL that can be shared around as necessary.

A theological/technological experiment by Blair Wang. Built on jQuery, Bootstrap, and Font Awesome.
